Rhythms

Rhythms are 2 jugglers patterns that can be used has the has the rhythm part of many Multi-Siteswap patterns.

All Rhythm patterns use a simple 2 juggler layout;

Basic Counts

Number of Jugglers: 2

Number of Objects: 6

This covers a broad number of rhythms all based on 2 siteswaps exchanging objects at a regular interval based on the number of throws, or counts, between an exchange. For example; The most common rhythm used is "4 count" which is:

P S S S

Basic counts alway straight passes and siteswaps always alternate between right and left hand. Therefore;

  • Even counts - The pass will always be thrown from the same hand.
  • Odd counts - The pass will alternate between which hand throws it.

Fast Slow

Number of Jugglers: 2

Number of Objects: 6

This is really a set of rhythms. The basic rule are;

  • Each juggler has a different rhythm one basic count apart, in this example A will be juggling a 2 count rhythm and B will be juggling a 1 count rhythm
  • The juggler with the even rhythm, normally the fast part, alternates Pass and Diagonal throws alternating between the hands of the other juggler
  • The juggler with the odd rhythm, normally the slow part, alternates Diagonal and Pass throws so all throws to the other jugglers same hand

A: P S D S
B: P D

Notes: Juggler B starts with a diagonal that will come from the hand that Juggler A's Pass is to. Assuming right hand start, Juggler B's first throw will be a Diagonal from the left hand. This means the timing Juggler B's Diagonal throws must be in front of Juggler A's Diagonal throws.

Feeds

Feeds are patterns of at least 3 jugglers. Normally one juggler is declared has the "feeder" (there could be multiple feeders) who’s job is the feed objects all other juggler. All "feedees" generally only exchange objects with the feeder.

In general the feeder's count is the same has the feedee's, except multiplied by the number of feedees, i.e 2 feedees passing in 4-count, means the Feeder is passing in 2-count. If there are more than 2 feedees, they will pass at whatever the rate the feeder can feed them at.

When there are more than 2 feedees, there are 2 common orders in which they get feed;

  • Sweep - The feeder sweeps back and forth across the line feedees. This is the most common
  • Typewriter - The feeder goes down the line in one direction, at the end of the line they go back to the start

Dave and Ralph's Endless Patter Pattern

Two or More People (People Can Come And Go At Will - And They Usually Do)

Any Number Of Clubs (It Is Important To Have Some Clubs So That People Coming Into The "Pattern" Think That Juggling Will Be Starting Soon, But Under No Circumstances Should Any Clubs Be Thrown While Doing This Pattern)

P - Straight Pass

D - Diagonal Pass (Could Also Mean Double - And Sometimes means Double-Diagonal)

S - Self (or single)

H - Hand Across (also hold, hurry, or hairbrained-idea)

N - Nod (as in agreement - usually by two or more people agreeing to go somewhere else after they realize that the patter is actually the pattern)

A - Argument (We are not argling!)

NC - Notation Change (It is important to change the meaning of notation in the middle of the conversation)

T - Transition, Taxing, Tempting, ...

Right Handed Start (Usually - Sometimes You Can Start In The Middle Of The Pattern and Then You Might Start With The Left Hand)

Now Try Any Combination Of The Above.
